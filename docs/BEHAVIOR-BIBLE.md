# BloomBay Behavior Bible v1

> *"Trust is built slowly and destroyed instantly."*

**Status:** Living document.
**Last updated:** June 2026

---

## What This Document Is

The Behavior Bible governs how BloomBay responds to what women do inside it. Not rules — dynamics. Not punishment — consequence. Not gamification — culture.

The central question: *what does BloomBay reward, and what does it quietly discourage?*

---

## The Trust Ledger

Every woman on BloomBay has three invisible scores. They are not displayed as numbers on her profile. They are felt — in which events she gets invited to, which clubs surface her profile, how Yande speaks to her.

### 1. Bloom Trust Score
*Built through consistency. Destroyed through flakiness.*

**What raises it:**
- Attending a gathering she RSVP'd to
- Completing her profile with a real photo
- Being verified successfully
- Responding to Bloom Requests (accepting or declining)
- Staying in clubs she joined (30+ days of activity)
- Hosting a gathering that gets strong reviews

**What lowers it:**
- RSVP'ing and not attending (no-show)
- Multiple no-shows within 60 days → seat reservation capability suspended
- Blocking another woman within 48 hours of attending the same event
- Being flagged by a curator for conduct
- Leaving a club within 7 days of joining

**The quiet consequence:** A woman with low trust sees fewer open seats, fewer personal invitations, and Yande stops making specific suggestions (Yande only commits to women who commit back).

**She is never told her score.** She is simply invited to fewer things.

### 2. Attendance Score
*The most specific signal of real presence.*

Measures: gatherings attended / gatherings RSVP'd, trailing 90 days.

- 90%+ → Green status. First access to new gatherings.
- 70–89% → Standard. No disadvantage.
- 50–69% → Yellow. No auto-invitations. Curators may reach out.
- Below 50% → Seat reservation requires deposit increase.

Clubs that lose attendance fall in the health score. Members who attend gatherings keep clubs alive.

### 3. Community Score
*The signal of contribution.*

Raised by: posting in The Room, responding to bulletin questions, welcoming new members (curator action), writing restaurant/venue reviews, hosting in the Celebrate section.

Lowered by: being reported for harassment, aggressive messaging, gatekeeping in The Room.

High community score → eligible to become a Curator. This is the highest volunteer role in BloomBay.

## What Actions Are Rewarded

### For Members

**Showing up**
The most rewarded behavior in BloomBay. Every gathering attended increases access. A woman who shows up consistently becomes visible to Yande as someone worth investing in.

Reward: early access to new gatherings, priority seat selection, Yande suggestions become more specific and accurate.

**Joining a club and staying**
Clubs grow through consistency. A member who has been in a club for 90+ days is considered a "core member" — her presence anchors new members.

Reward: her profile is shown to new members as someone to connect with. She gets a "founding" badge on her crest if she was among the first 20 in the club.

**Completing the IRL funnel**
The 14-day funnel: join → onboard → pick a club → attend a gathering. Women who complete this within 14 days of joining are called Cohort-14.

Reward: elevated trust immediately. Yande unlocks the full suggestion engine. Access to the full Happenings feed.

**Sending a Bloom Request that gets accepted**
A successful connection (mutual acceptance) raises her community score and unlocks the ability to invite someone to a gathering as a guest.

**Writing real reviews**
Restaurant reviews, venue reviews, curator picks — written in the member's actual voice, not star ratings.

Reward: review is featured as a "Girl Gem" in Explore. Her handle appears on the gem permanently.

**Referring another woman who joins and attends**
Not a referral code. A woman shares the app with someone she knows. That person joins, attends an event, and the connection is traced.

Reward: both women's Bouquets are pre-suggested to each other. Yande notes the shared connection.

### For Club Owners

**Hosting consistent gatherings**
A club without gatherings is a waiting room. Clubs that host regularly gain health score and surface prominently in discovery.

Reward: priority placement in the Clubs tab. Automatic invitations sent to compatible members.

**Accepting applications thoughtfully**
A club owner who reviews and approves applications within 48 hours, with thoughtful messages, receives a "responsive host" signal.

Reward: applications from more experienced Bloomies are routed to high-signal hosts first.

**Keeping members engaged**
A club whose members post in The Room, RSVP to gatherings, and rate events highly is considered healthy.

Reward: Zone expansion eligibility. The club can branch to new neighborhoods.

**Mentoring new clubs**
Experienced club owners who mentor hosts in new cities (via the Yande/curator system) receive a "Founding Host" designation.

### For Curators

**Welcoming new members at gatherings**
A curator's primary role is the physical handoff — meeting a new member at her first gathering, making her feel expected.

Reward: payout per successful onboarding (tracked by Yande state). Access to Curator-only events. Community score boost.

**Flagging good content**
When a curator highlights a Wall Note, bulletin post, or review as worth surfacing — and that content generates positive engagement — her curatorial judgment score rises.

**Hosting Bulletin responses**
Curators who answer Bulletin questions quickly and helpfully surface as "trusted voices" in their neighborhood.

## What Is Quietly Discouraged

BloomBay does not punish through notifications or warnings (with one exception: safety violations). It discourages through consequence — access narrows, visibility shrinks, suggestions stop.

### No-Shows
The single most damaging behavior in the ecosystem. A woman who says she'll be somewhere and doesn't show makes the table worse for everyone who did show.

First no-show: note in attendance score.
Second within 30 days: Yande stops suggesting seats to her.
Third within 60 days: seat reservation suspended for 30 days.
After suspension: she can return. The score resets slowly.

### Leaving a Club Immediately After Joining
Joining a club and leaving within 7 days is treated as noise — it suggests the join was casual and the club was not what she expected.

Consequence: that club is not shown to her again for 30 days. She cannot rejoin for 14 days.

### Sending Bloom Requests and Never Responding
If a woman sends multiple Bloom Requests and then doesn't respond to incoming ones, Yande reads this as passive networking — not genuine connection.

Consequence: fewer incoming Bloom Requests are routed to her.

### Bulk-Joining Many Clubs
Joining more than seven clubs in a 30-day window triggers a signal. The clubs are not removed, but Yande does not assist her in engaging with them — it waits for natural selection.

### Posting Without Engaging
In The Room, a woman who posts frequently but never responds to others' posts is a broadcaster, not a community member.

Consequence: her posts surface slightly lower in The Wall ranking. (She will never be told this.)

### Aggressive or Gatekeeping Behavior
Any form of exclusion, condescension, or repeated reporting against other members triggers a curator review.

If confirmed: access to The Room restricted to read-only for 7 days. Second offense: account review by the founding team.

## How Friendships Form

BloomBay does not have a "friends" button. Friendship emerges through a sequence of signals that Yande tracks and responds to.

### Stage 1: Proximity
Two women attend the same gathering. Yande notes the shared context: event, time, table proximity.

### Stage 2: Signal
One woman sends the other a Bloom Request. The request surfaces the shared context: "You were both at Saturday in SoHo."

### Stage 3: Acceptance
The Bloom Request is accepted. Both women are placed in each other's "In Your Orbit" list in the Connect tab.

### Stage 4: Engagement
They interact in The Room, or plan something together via the Planner, or attend another gathering at the same table.

After three positive interactions (gatherings, Planner events, or Room exchanges), Yande considers the friendship established and may suggest them to each other's Bouquet.

### Stage 5: Bouquet
The Bouquet holds a maximum of twelve women. Adding someone to your Bouquet is intentional — it is a signal that this woman matters to you specifically.

Bouquet members see each other's plans first. They get priority at the same table when seat-selecting. Yande routes them to the same gatherings.

### The Bouquet Limit

Twelve is not arbitrary. The Dunbar number suggests 150 meaningful relationships — the Bouquet is the inner 12. BloomBay does not reward a Bouquet of 200 women who never meet. It rewards depth.

## How Clubs Grow

A club is not a static community. It is a living organism. It can grow, plateau, rest, and revive.

### Club Life Stages

**Emerging (0–50 members)**
New club. Host is setting culture. First gatherings are intimate — 6–10 women. Yande actively routes relevant women to the club's page.

Behaviors that advance: consistent gatherings (at least monthly), active The Room presence, approved applications within 48 hours.

**Active (50–200 members)**
The club has established a rhythm. Members self-recruit. Health score is the primary metric.

Behaviors that advance: Zone applications (geographic expansion), mentoring new members, hosting events with high attendance scores.

**Established (200–500 members)**
A landmark club. Surfaces prominently in city discovery. May have multiple Zones (neighborhood chapters).

Behaviors that sustain: cultural consistency (the club is the same club it was at 50 members), hosting flagship quarterly gatherings, graduating members to co-host roles.

**Legendary (500+)**
Rare. A club that has been active for 12+ months with consistent health. Receives a "Bloom Landmark" designation on its page.

**Resting**
A club that has not hosted a gathering in 45+ days. Not deleted — resting. Shown with a quiet "resting" indicator. Host can revive it by scheduling a gathering and reaching out to the 10 most active former members.

**Dissolved**
By host's choice, or by BloomBay if the club has been inactive for 6+ months and has fewer than 20 members. Member crests from dissolved clubs remain in their collections.

### Zone Expansion

A club expands to a new neighborhood by submitting a Zone Request. Requires:
- 200+ members in primary location
- A named Zone Lead (an established club member who will anchor the new neighborhood)
- Curator approval

Zone Leads are not paid. They receive: elevated community score, Zone Lead crest, first access to new gathering slots in their neighborhood.

## How Gatherings Work

A gathering is the atomic unit of BloomBay. Everything — clubs, the app, trust scores, friendships — ultimately exists to fill a table.

### The Anatomy of a Gathering

**Pre-gathering:**
1. Club host or BloomBay team creates a gathering with date, venue, capacity, dress code, vibe.
2. Open seats are listed. Each seat has a deposit ($10–$95 depending on type).
3. Yande routes personalized invitations to compatible women.
4. Women RSVP. Their attendance scores are visible to the host.
5. Host finalizes the table 48 hours before.

**Day of:**
1. A Curator (if assigned) arrives at the venue 20 minutes early.
2. She has a printed list of attendees and their names.
3. She greets each woman at the door. She knows something specific about each one (from her profile).
4. She introduces women who should know each other.
5. She quietly ensures no one is sitting alone or struggling to find their place.

**Post-gathering:**
1. Women can mark each other as connections (triggering the Bloom orbit system).
2. Host submits a recap (optional: Polaroid, voice note, photo).
3. Attendance is logged. Trust scores update.
4. 48 hours later: Yande checks if any connections were formed and surfaces those women to each other.

### Types of Gatherings

**Open Seat Dinner**
The flagship. A curated table at a vetted restaurant. 6–12 women. Deposit required. Dress code posted. Host curates the table in advance. Yande suggests compatible attendees.

**Club Gathering**
A gathering hosted by and for a specific club. Members get first access. Open seats may be offered to the wider BloomBay community.

**Blueday (All-Member)**
Quarterly gatherings for the entire BloomBay community in a city. All members eligible. No deposit. Informal. The city-wide pulse-check.

**Solo Gathering**
Not an oxymoron. Solo happenings are curated activities (museum visits, yoga classes, coffee-shop mornings) where a woman might encounter other BloomBay members without the formality of a dinner. No RSVP required. Yande notes if two solo plans overlap.

**Founding Mothers Dinner**
Exclusive to Founding Mothers and their guests. Quarterly. No deposit. Hosted by a founding team member. The founding covenant table.

### Seating Logic

Seats are not assigned randomly. The seating algorithm (run by Yande) considers:
- Interests alignment (max 3 shared interest areas at a table)
- Neighborhood proximity (women who live nearby tend to become recurring friends)
- Energy balance (introverts and extroverts are mixed)
- New-member presence (at least one Founding Mother or established member per table if a new member attends)
- Previous table history (two women who were at the same table recently are not placed together again for 60 days unless they specifically connect)

**Chemistry score:** Shown on the seat detail page. Calculated from Yande's profile analysis. Displayed as a percentage. Never shown below 70% (BloomBay only surfaces strong potential connections).

## How Trust Works

Trust in BloomBay operates on three levels: institutional trust (BloomBay → member), interpersonal trust (member → member), and communal trust (member → community).

### Institutional Trust (BloomBay → Member)

BloomBay earns trust through:
- **Verification that means something.** Every woman is photo-verified. Every gathering has a verified attendee list. The door is not open to everyone.
- **Deposits that are honored.** If a gathering is cancelled, deposits are refunded within 48 hours, no questions asked.
- **Privacy that is absolute.** A Bloom Request decline is never communicated to the sender. Blocking is invisible. Safety reports go directly to the founding team.
- **Yande who speaks honestly.** If there are no good matches, Yande says nothing rather than suggesting a poor match. Silence is trust.

### Interpersonal Trust (Member → Member)

Member trust is built through presence, not promises. The system does not ask women to vouch for each other. It observes:
- Did they show up to the same gathering?
- Did they both post in The Room this week?
- Do they have overlapping clubs?

A woman who shares three gatherings with another woman without them connecting is shown in her "people you keep crossing paths with" list by Yande.

### Communal Trust (Member → Community)

The community trusts itself when individuals contribute to it:
- A Bulletin question answered by three different women creates a resource.
- A Restaurant review added to the girl gems becomes permanent infrastructure.
- A Wall Note that helps someone find an apartment is a real act of community.

Community trust is fragile. One high-profile incident of harassment, exclusion, or dishonesty damages it for everyone. This is why:
- Curatorial response time is 24 hours or less.
- Founding team reviews any incident flagged by three or more members.
- Women who cause harm are quietly removed — no announcement, no drama.

## The Curator Role

Curators are the human infrastructure of BloomBay's trust system. They are not moderators (a defensive role). They are welcomers (an active role).

### What Curators Do

1. **Welcome new members at gatherings** — The physical handshake. Show up. Know the room. Introduce women who should know each other.

2. **Surface community content** — In The Room, curators flag Wall Notes and bulletin posts worth highlighting. Their curatorial judgment shapes what new members see first.

3. **Host neighborhood check-ins** — Monthly Bulletin posts asking: "What does this neighborhood need more of?" The responses inform future gathering locations.

4. **Alert founding team** — If something is wrong (a member feeling unsafe, a gathering going poorly, a club host behaving badly), the curator is the first point of contact.

### How Curators Are Selected

You cannot apply to be a Curator. You become one by having a high community score, attending 8+ gatherings, and being nominated by a founding team member or another Curator.

The invitation arrives as a Bloom Request — not a job offer. "Yande thinks you'd be good at this."

### What Curators Receive

- Payout per successful member onboarding (tracked by Yande state)
- Access to the Curator portal (gathering assignments, payout history, women roster)
- A Curator crest on their profile (visible to all members)
- Priority seat at all Founding Mothers dinners
- City-specific community budget ($50/month for neighborhood notes, welcome gifts, etc.)

## The Safety System

BloomBay is a women-only space. This is not a feature — it is the foundation. The safety system exists to protect it.

### Verification

Every member submits a profile photo before gaining full access. The founding team reviews photos within 24 hours. This is not automated — it is human review.

Verification criteria:
- Real face visible (not AI-generated, not logo, not pet)
- Photo consistency with stated profile (age range, general appearance)
- No red flags (public figures using alias, previously flagged accounts)

A verified member receives a verification badge visible only to curatorial staff, not other members. Members see each other as humans, not verified/unverified statuses.

### The Safety Report

Any member can report another member. The report goes directly to the founding team (not to any other member or curator).

Reports are investigated within 24 hours. If confirmed:
- First offense (verbal harassment, unwanted contact): 7-day restriction
- Second offense: permanent ban
- Physical safety concern: immediate ban + optional law enforcement guidance

### The Girl Code

Each member sets her own Girl Code — her values and boundaries as a BloomBay member. This is visible on her Lounge page (private) but informs how Yande routes connections.

A member who sets "no men in my social life" will not be connected with women who are primarily looking for couples' friends. A member who sets "I'm new and nervous" will be paired with a Curator at her first gathering.

The Girl Code is never revealed to other members. It is Yande's context.

## The IRL Funnel (14-Day Window)

The most important behavioral sequence in BloomBay. Women who complete the 14-day funnel stay. Women who do not, drift.

### The Funnel

```
Day 0:    Join + complete profile (name, photo, location, interests)
Day 1-3:  Choose at least one club
Day 3-7:  RSVP to a gathering or open seat
Day 7-14: Attend the gathering
```

### What Happens If She Doesn't Complete

**Day 3, no club selected:** Yande sends a single nudge. "Three clubs match your profile. You might love one." No further nudges unless she opens the app.

**Day 7, no RSVP:** A Curator may send a welcome message through the Bulletin. Not a notification — a human-authored community post she might see.

**Day 14, no attendance:** She remains a member with full access but Yande enters "resting mode" — stops personalizing, stops suggesting. She will see the generic community feed.

**Day 30, no attendance:** She receives a single email: "Still in? We're saving a seat." One link to an upcoming gathering. If she doesn't click, she is placed in the waitlist pool for her city's next Blueday (all-member) gathering — a lower-stakes re-entry.

### What Happens When She Completes

The moment she attends her first gathering:
- Attendance score initializes at 100%
- Trust score receives its first positive signal
- Yande exits "onboarding mode" and begins full personalization
- Her city's founding team is notified (a number on their Cohort-14 dashboard)
- She receives access to the Founding Mothers dinner waitlist
